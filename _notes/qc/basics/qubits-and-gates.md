A qubit is a unit vector in a two-dimensional complex vector space.

Key ideas:

- Measurement returns classical outcomes with probabilities.
- Gates are unitary operations that evolve the state.
- Superposition and phase drive quantum behavior.

Common beginner mistake: treating amplitudes as probabilities directly. Probabilities come from squared magnitudes.
